The Question

Does the HECM line of credit outperform a fully invested portfolio as a retirement buffer?

The 4% rule exists because of one problem: sequence of returns risk. If markets decline early in retirement, a retiree who sells assets to fund income locks in losses at the worst possible moment. The portfolio never fully recovers. This is the core threat the HECM line of credit is designed to neutralize.

We tested this across two distinct market environments using actual historical returns — a worst-case sequence of returns period, and a more representative 21-year cycle that includes both crashes and a prolonged bull market. The same client, the same assumptions, the same three paths. The data is fully sourced and shown in the addendum.

The Other Risk Nobody
Plans For

Sequence of returns isn't the only threat to retirement income. Sudden large expenses — a health event, a home, a family emergency — can devastate a portfolio that has no separate buffer. New 2026 research by Genda, Neuwirth, Sacks & Sacks examines what happens when spike expenses arrive at the wrong time.

What Is a Spike Expense?

A spike expense is a sudden, large, irregular cost that falls outside the normal retirement income plan. Think: a major medical event, a long-term care bridge, an emergency home repair, or a family obligation. Unlike regular inflation-adjusted withdrawals, spikes are unpredictable in both size and timing.

The Sacks research (2026) demonstrates that spike timing is at least as dangerous as market timing. A spike in years 5–6 of retirement, when the portfolio is still building momentum, can permanently reduce sustainable withdrawal rates by nearly 20% — from 9.1% to 7.4% of the initial portfolio in their modeled scenarios.

Three Strategies, Three Outcomes

Reserve Strategy

The HECM LOC is held in reserve from day one. Never touched unless a spike occurs. The line grows untouched, building capacity over time.

Lowest depletion risk

Coordinated Strategy

The LOC and portfolio are used together — LOC during down years and spikes, portfolio during good years. Balanced legacy and security.

Best balance

Last Resort Strategy

Portfolio is used first. LOC only accessed when the portfolio is exhausted. This is what most retirees do by default — and it performs worst.

Highest depletion risk

The upfront costs of the HECM — which are not out-of-pocket — can be thought of as an insurance premium against spike expenses. The line grows whether used or not. When a spike arrives, the portfolio never has to absorb it.
